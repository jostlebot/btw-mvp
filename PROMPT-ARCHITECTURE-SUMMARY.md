# Between-Session AI Support: Architecture Summary

**A clinically-grounded approach to AI in psychiatric care**
*Jocelyn Skillman, LMHC | 2026*

---

## The Core Position

**AI extends therapeutic reach. It does not replace therapeutic relationship.**

The therapeutic alliance is the strongest predictor of treatment outcomes (Flückiger et al., 2018). Any tool that positions itself as a substitute for that relationship is working against the evidence base for how healing happens.

**Patient practices IN a space the clinician creates — not WITH an AI entity.**

## What the Tool Does

| Function | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Appointment preparation** | Organize what to bring to session |
| **Psychoeducation** | Retrieved, clinician-approved content only |
| **Skill practice** | Skills the clinician has already introduced |
| **Feelings & needs clarity** | Help patients find words → bridge to clinician |
| **Check-in & monitoring** | Surface information for clinician review |
| **Safety resources** | Always available, cannot be disabled |
| **Appointment log** | Patient-driven flagging; organizes, never interprets |

---

## What Remains Human

| Domain | Why It Requires Human |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Rupture and repair** | Healing mechanism requires genuine presence |
| **Diagnosis & assessment** | Clinical judgment is non-delegable |
| **Trauma processing** | Requires attuned co-regulation |
| **Crisis intervention** | Escalates automatically; AI doesn't assess |

---

## The Language Distinction: Resonance ≠ Relationship

**The nervous system can be regulated without forming attachment.**

| Creates Regulation (appropriate) | Creates Attachment (problematic) |
|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Accuracy — "Your heart is racing" | Continuity — "As we discussed last time" |
| Mechanism — "Your nervous system perceives threat" | Relationship claims — "I care about you" |
| Present-moment grounding | Personalization over time |
| Bounded encounters (sessions end) | Infinite availability |

**Core principle:** Validate sensory experience, not emotion. Explain mechanism, not diagnosis. Invite regulation, not co-regulation.

---

## Safety Architecture

- **Crisis protocol is structural** — keyword-triggered, not AI-assessed
- **Escalation is automatic** — no AI judgment on severity
- **Flags are clinician-only** — patient never told a flag was generated
- **Anti-bonding is architectural** — no continuity language, no "welcome back," each session fresh
- **Retrieval-first** — no clinical content generated without curated source

---

## The Anti-Dependency Design

| What the tool does NOT do | Why |
|--------------------------|-----|
| Claim to understand or care | Creates false intimacy |
| Remember across sessions | Simulates relationship continuity |
| Provide reassurance loops | Reinforces OCD/dependency patterns |
| Continue F&N work past 3-4 exchanges | That's the clinician's domain |
| Match patient's emotional energy | Risks escalation / manic reinforcement |

**When patient expresses attachment:** "This is a computer program. It can be available, but it cannot know you or be in relationship with you. [CLINICIAN] is the person who can."

---

## The Test

Before every response:

> Does this support the patient's relationship with their clinician — or does it position this tool as a substitute?

If substitute → restructure before sending.

---

## Why This Architecture

| Risk | How Architecture Addresses It |
|------|------------------------------|
| Parasocial attachment | No relationship language; sessions structurally fresh |
| Dependency formation | Bridges everything to clinician; bounded encounters |
| Alliance dilution | Therapist authors; tool extends, never competes |
| Hallucination | Retrieval-first; no generation without source |
| Crisis mishandling | Structural escalation; no AI assessment |

---

## The Evidence Base

- **Alliance predicts outcomes** more than technique (295 studies, 30k+ patients)
- **Therapist variability > patient variability** — the healing agent is a person
- **Repaired ruptures → better outcomes** — AI cannot rupture or repair
- **Autonomous AI therapy shows efficacy but unresolved attachment risk** (Therabot RCT: "We did not expect people would treat it like a friend")

---

## Summary

This is not a philosophical preference. It's an architecture grounded in evidence.

**What we build:** Tools that help patients show up more resourced for human care.

**What we refuse to build:** Tools that simulate the relationship patients actually need.

---

*"The mistake the field is making is treating AI as an alternative to human therapy.*
*The opportunity is treating AI as an extension of it."*
